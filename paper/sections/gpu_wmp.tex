\section{GPU WMP evaluator}
\label{sec:gpu_wmp}

WMP evaluation is compute-dense (Faddeeva + broadened
curvefit + pole sum) with a small working set (few KB of
pole data per nuclide), which matches GPU arithmetic
pipelines well. We ported the CPU evaluator to CUDA as
\texttt{\_\_device\_\_} helpers in
\texttt{gpu/cuda/transport.cu} and added a
\texttt{wmp\_test\_eval} kernel plus a host validator binary
\texttt{rust\_prototype/src/bin/gpu\_wmp\_validate.rs}.

\paragraph{Bit-exactness.}
CPU-vs-GPU over twelve U-$238$ test energies at $T = 293.6$~K:
maximum relative discrepancy $5.3 \cdot 10^{-14}$ on
absorption and $2.0 \cdot 10^{-13}$ on fission --- double
precision round-off only.

\paragraph{Throughput on RTX~$3080$.}
On a $10^6$-energy log-spaced grid inside U-$238$'s RRR:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{@{}lrr@{}}
\toprule
Implementation & ns/lookup & Relative \\
\midrule
CPU single-thread (Ryzen~$9800$X3D) & $66.8$ & $1.00\times$ \\
GPU (RTX~$3080$, saturated)         & $9.5$  & $0.14\times$ \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

$7.0\times$ per-lookup GPU-vs-CPU.

\paragraph{Standalone GPU evaluator only.}
The validation above is for the GPU evaluator on its own,
exercised by a host driver that broadcasts a fixed energy
grid to all threads and reads back the cross-section vector.
Wiring the evaluator into the event-based
\texttt{transport\_persistent} kernel for end-to-end GPU
hybrid transport requires a bitmap \texttt{has\_wmp[i]}
plus per-nuclide $E_{\min}^{\mathrm{WMP}}, E_{\max}^{\mathrm{WMP}}$
checks inline, the recursive Humlicek conjugate fold unrolled
to a flag-plus-sign-flip form (recursive
\verb|__device__| calls spill to local memory under the
register pressure of \texttt{\_\_launch\_bounds\_\_(256,\,2)}
and trigger CUDA\_ERROR\_ILLEGAL\_ADDRESS on deep stacks),
and a recomputed total cross section from partials with URR
sampling suppressed inside the WMP window. We have not
completed the in-kernel integration and report no in-engine
GPU hybrid $k_\infty$ or throughput.
