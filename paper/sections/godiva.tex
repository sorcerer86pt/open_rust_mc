\section{Godiva: validation against the ICSBEP experiment}
\label{sec:godiva}

Godiva (HEU-MET-FAST-$001$) is a bare $93.71\%$-enriched
uranium metal sphere. Its fast, leakage-dominated spectrum
places no demand on the resolved-resonance region. The
ICSBEP benchmark value is the experimental critical mass,
$k_\text{eff}^\text{exp} = 1.0000 \pm 0.0010$
(1~$\sigma$)~\cite{icsbep_hmf001}. This is a \emph{physical}
benchmark with a measured uncertainty, and it is the
criterion against which the engine is validated in this
section. We report OpenMC~$0.15.3$ run on the same
ENDF/B-VII.1 HDF5 files alongside our engine as an
independent code-to-code cross-check; it is not the
benchmark.

Table~\ref{tab:keff_godiva_desktop} reports the final
post-fix four-provider benchmark on the large-L3 system at
rank-$5$. Table~\ref{tab:keff_godiva_fixes} traces the
three transport corrections that move the engine from
$+325$~pcm to $+79$~pcm vs ICSBEP.
Table~\ref{tab:keff_godiva_ranksweep} reports the earlier
pre-fix rank sweep retained for spectrum illustration;
small-L3 five-seed results are in Table~\ref{tab:keff_godiva}.

\subsection{Three transport fixes that closed $325 \to 79$~pcm}
\label{sec:godiva_fixes}

A channel-level comparison against OpenMC on the same
HDF5 files localised the original pre-fix
$k_\text{eff} = 1.00325$ (\emph{pre-fix} here is a
historical baseline, not the shipped engine) to a sampling
issue in transport kinematics rather than to library data:
Rust produced $+0.87\%$ more fissions per source neutron,
$+0.41\%$ more collisions, and $-0.68\%$ less leakage than
OpenMC, a signature consistent with ``scattering not
forward-peaked enough, particles stay inside longer''. A
static XS diff confirmed fission, elastic, inelastic, and
$\bar\nu$ agreed with OpenMC's Python API to $<\!0.2\%$
across the Godiva energy range. XS magnitudes were not the
bug; three transport sampling bugs were.

\paragraph{Fix~$1$: (n,$2$n) and (n,$3$n) secondary banking
($-106$~pcm).} Secondary neutrons from MT$=16$ and MT$=17$
were appended to the next generation's fission source bank.
Since $k_\text{eff}$ is measured from
$\lvert\text{bank}\rvert/N_\text{source}$, every (n,$2$n)
added one fake fission neutron to the $k$ estimator.
Measured (n,$2$n) rate on Godiva is $0.00253$/source, an
upper-bound contribution of $+253$~pcm if every secondary
contaminated the $k$ estimator at unit weight; the measured
shift after routing secondaries through in-generation
transport is $-106$~pcm because a
properly-transported secondary produces $\approx\!k\approx 1$
fission neutron per current-generation transport at
equilibrium, which partially replaces the direct banking
contribution. The fix introduces a per-source secondary
stack drained inside the history loop with a shared
\texttt{max\_events} budget bounding pathological cascades.

\paragraph{Fix~$2$: $\mu$-CDF inversion for linear-linear
ENDF angular distributions ($-31$~pcm).} ENDF/B-VII.1 stores
all $49$ U-$235$ elastic-scatter incident-energy
distributions with \texttt{interpolation = 2} (linear-linear
PDF, quadratic CDF within each bin). Our tabular sampler
treated every distribution as histogram, producing a
piecewise-linear CDF and consistently under-sampling forward
peaks. The fix reads the \texttt{interpolation} attribute
from HDF5 and, for linear-linear bins, inverts the
quadratic CDF by solving $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$ for the
physical root in $[0, \Delta\mu]$ (same convention as
OpenMC's \texttt{Tabular::sample}). Histogram bins keep the
existing linear path.

\paragraph{Fix~$3$: MT$=91$ continuum inelastic tabulated
outgoing-energy distribution ($-98$~pcm, largest single
shift).} Our continuum-inelastic branch sampled outgoing
neutron energy from an evaporation spectrum with nuclear
temperature $T = \sqrt{E^\star/a}$, $a = A/8$~MeV$^{-1}$.
OpenMC, MCNP, and Serpent sample directly from the ENDF
MT$=91$ tabulated distribution stored at
\texttt{reaction\_091/product\_0/distribution\_0/}
\texttt{\{energy,distribution\}}. On Godiva's fast spectrum
the evaporation model produced a
harder-than-true secondary spectrum, keeping neutrons in the
fast-fission regime and inflating $k_\text{eff}$. The fix
loads the tabulated distribution at engine start and falls
back to evaporation only for nuclides where the HDF5 file
lacks it. MT$=16$ and MT$=17$ receive the same ENDF-tabulated
treatment for an additional $-11$~pcm (within seed noise).

\paragraph{Ruling out a library-level explanation.}
A candidate explanation for the pre-fix $+325$~pcm offset
was a residual ENDF/B-VII.1 Godiva overprediction of
$+300$--$500$~pcm reported in some earlier
evaluations~\cite{icsbep_hmf001}. Running OpenMC $0.15.3$
on the same HDF5 files used by our engine yields
$k_\text{eff} = 0.99901$, i.e.\ $\Delta_\text{exp} = -99$~pcm
(within $1\sigma_\text{exp}$). A $+300$--$500$~pcm
ENDF/B-VII.1 bias is therefore not observed at the
statistics of this measurement, which localises the
pre-fix $+325$~pcm Rust offset to the transport engine
rather than to the evaluated library. The three fixes
above resolve the engine-level contribution. After them
both engines ($+79$ and $-99$~pcm) validate against
ICSBEP inside its experimental uncertainty from opposite
sides, without requiring any library correction.

The two post-fix CPU providers agree on $k_\text{eff}$ to
within $5$~pcm ($\ll\!1\,\mathrm{SEM}_\text{combined}$).
Both sit inside the ICSBEP experimental uncertainty band
of $\pm\,100$~pcm. OpenMC $0.15.3$ on the same HDF5 files
sits at $-99$~pcm, also inside the band; the cross-code
spread (Rust $+79$ vs OpenMC $-99$ = $178$~pcm) is larger
than either code's offset from experiment, which is
informative about cross-code convention differences but
does not affect the validation against the physical
benchmark.

On the large-L3 system Godiva CPU SVD rank-$5$ at
$820$~ns/p is $1.40\times$ faster than the CPU pointwise
table at $1\,150$~ns/p. The GPU rows from the earlier
pre-fix runs are retained below for throughput context
only; re-measuring them post-fix is future work because
porting fix~$3$ (MT$=91$ tabulated) to the CUDA kernel is
not yet complete. On the small-L3 system (pre-fix,
Table~\ref{tab:keff_godiva}), SVD rank-$2$ was $2.8\times$
faster than the pointwise table at a modest $75$~pcm bias
relative to its own pointwise baseline.
