\section{Godiva rank sweep}
\label{sec:godiva}

Godiva (HEU-MET-FAST-001) is a bare $93.71\%$-enriched
uranium metal sphere. Its fast, leakage-dominated spectrum
places no demand on the RRR. The ICSBEP benchmark value is
$k_\text{eff}^\text{exp} = 1.0000 \pm 0.0010$
(1~$\sigma$)~\cite{icsbep_hmf001}.
Table~\ref{tab:keff_godiva_desktop} reports the post-correction
four-provider ten-seed benchmark on the large-L3 system at
rank-$5$; Table~\ref{tab:keff_godiva_ranksweep} reports the
pre-correction rank sweep retained for spectrum illustration;
small-L3 five-seed results are in Table~\ref{tab:keff_godiva}.

All four post-correction providers agree on $k_\text{eff}$
to within $37$~pcm
($\approx 1.4\,\mathrm{SEM}_\text{combined}$). They cluster
$325$--$362$~pcm \emph{above} the ICSBEP benchmark, which is
the known ENDF/B-VII.1 Godiva bias: evaluated-library
re-runs of HEU-MET-FAST-001 with the same ENDF/B-VII.1
nuclide data systematically over-predict $k_\text{eff}$ by
$\sim\!300$--$500$~pcm, closing to $\sim\!50$~pcm under
ENDF/B-VIII.0~\cite{icsbep_hmf001}. Our engine reproduces
that library-level bias and does not add an engine-level
offset to it.

On the large-L3 system Godiva CPU SVD rank-$5$ is
$1.43\times$ faster than the CPU pointwise table
($846$ vs $1\,207$~ns/p). The pre-correction rank sweep
(Table~\ref{tab:keff_godiva_ranksweep}) inverts this ordering
because the sampler change cleared an unrelated per-particle
cost in SVD mode; we report both for reproducibility.
GPU pointwise at $302$~ns/p is $4.0\times$ the large-L3 CPU
table; GPU SVD rank-$5$ at $378$~ns/p is $1.25\times$ slower
than GPU pointwise. On the small-L3 system (pre-correction,
Table~\ref{tab:keff_godiva}), SVD rank-$2$ was $2.8\times$
faster than the pointwise table at a modest $75$~pcm bias.
