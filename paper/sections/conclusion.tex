\section{Conclusion}

Truncated SVD of the $\log_{10}$-transformed cross-section
matrix is a defensible drop-in \emph{representation} for
the pointwise table. On a nine-nuclide thermal PWR pin cell,
the same engine running rank-$5$ SVD vs its own pointwise
table agrees on $k_\infty$ within $13$--$47$~pcm on two
hardware tiers, and all four in-engine providers (CPU table,
CPU SVD, GPU pointwise, GPU SVD) agree with
OpenMC~$0.15.3$ to $|\Delta| \le 51$~pcm at ten-seed
statistics after a transport-level bug audit closed an
initial $78$--$127$~pcm engine offset. A Humlicek-W4
Faddeeva evaluator, pure-Rust WMP reader, and SVD+WMP hybrid
provider all work end-to-end and reproduce $k_\infty$ within
two $\mathrm{SEM}$ of the matched SVD baseline at the same
software revision; a CUDA port of the WMP evaluator is
bit-exact against the CPU path and $7\times$ faster per
lookup on a consumer GPU.

On Godiva (HEU-MET-FAST-001) the four providers cluster
$325$--$362$~pcm \emph{above} the ICSBEP experimental value
$1.0000 \pm 0.0010$~\cite{icsbep_hmf001}. That offset is the
well-known ENDF/B-VII.1 Godiva bias --- library re-runs at
the same nuclide data systematically over-predict
$k_\text{eff}$ by $300$--$500$~pcm, closing to $\sim\!50$~pcm
under ENDF/B-VIII.0 --- so we read this as our engine
faithfully reproducing the library and not adding an
engine-level offset on top of it. The four providers agree
with each other on Godiva to within $37$~pcm, consistent
with the sub-SEM provider spread seen on PWR.

At a realistic \emph{off-library} operating temperature,
the story is more interesting. With a uniform $+150$~K
offset applied to every nuclide (fuel $900\!\to\!1050$~K,
moderator $600\!\to\!750$~K), OpenMC-style stochastic
$T$-picking enabled on the pointwise and hybrid paths, and
a $3$-point unity Ducru weight scheme on the SVD path
(Sec.~\ref{sec:ducru_puoi}), SVD reproduces $k_\infty$
within $213$~pcm of ACE+WMP at ten-seed $50\,000$-particle
$120$-batch statistics --- a small ($\sim\!3.6\sigma$) but
real systematic, inside the publishable band. SVD is
$22\%$ smaller than WMP in memory and matches its speed
within $0.07\%$. This is the result the paper exists to
report: at a realistic operating temperature a compressed
SVD representation of ENDF/B-VII.1 cross sections beats
the industry ACE+WMP baseline on memory, ties it on speed,
and trails it by a characterisable small systematic on
$k_\infty$ (Sec.~\ref{sec:off_library_3t}).

Getting there was non-trivial. A naive $2$-point unity Ducru
leaves a $2094$~pcm kernel-approximation bias at the same
operating conditions (Sec.~\ref{sec:off_library_2t}); a
simplex-projected QP that clips the signed Ducru weights
to non-negativity is \emph{worse} ($5\%$ L2 error on
U-$238$ capture versus $0.84\%$ for the signed $3$-point
scheme) because the simplex projection is Euclidean-optimal
in weight space whereas the raw Ducru weights are
Euclidean-optimal in the Doppler-kernel norm
(Sec.~\ref{sec:off_library_qp}). We also found and fixed a
channel-consistency bug in our stochastic $T$-pick: drawing
a fresh $\xi$ per partial channel produced non-monotonic
$k_\infty$-versus-$T$ behaviour that on-library measurements
cannot detect (Sec.~\ref{sec:off_library_bug}). The
PHYSOR-style off-library test surfaced it as a byproduct of
stressing the representation.

The \emph{performance} story on-library is less one-sided.
CPU SVD rank-$5$ is $1.37\times$--$1.90\times$ faster than
the in-engine pointwise table on PWR pin cell
(hardware-dependent) and $1.43\times$ faster than the table
on Godiva on the large-L3 system; GPU SVD is $1.30\times$ \emph{slower}
than GPU pointwise on PWR because four clad nuclides require
summing $13$ discrete-level SVD kernels per lookup.
The hybrid is $2.06\times$ slower than CPU SVD because
WMP evaluation is $\sim\!15\times$ more expensive than the
SVD FMA per lookup.

The \emph{memory} story is a correction of our earlier
messaging. The representation-byte ratio of
$132.9\times$ (WMP + smooth-SVD vs four-channel
six-temperature pointwise) is real; the in-engine
reduction we measured is $1.06\times$, and the hybrid
engine is absolutely $4.8$--$5.1\times$ larger than the
pointwise-table baseline. The discrete-level SVD kernels,
which WMP does not cover, dominate the engine's working
set. Reducing those kernels from rank $5$ to rank $1$ ---
the reviewer's suggestion and a free change because levels
are weakly $T$-dependent (Sec.~\ref{sec:discrete_rank}) ---
closes $\approx\!70\%$ of the engine's SVD working set on
Godiva with no measurable $k_\text{eff}$ shift. This is
what enables the off-library $22\%$ memory win against
ACE+WMP referenced above: with discrete-level rank $1$
applied, the SVD engine is absolutely smaller than the
two-endpoint stochastic-$T$ pointwise table even before
the WMP comparison.

The practical takeaway for a reader considering this
approach: rank-$5$ SVD with $3$-point unity Ducru
temperature interpolation and discrete-level rank-$1$ is
correct, agrees with OpenMC and its own pointwise baseline
within $\le 3\,\mathrm{SEM}_{10}$ on PWR at on-library $T$,
and agrees with ACE+WMP within $\sim\!3.6\,\mathrm{SEM}_{10}$
on PWR at off-library $T$. On memory it wins modestly
on-library and by $22\%$ off-library; on speed it is
$1.4\times$--$1.9\times$ faster than pointwise on CPU and
at parity with ACE+WMP off-library. The $43/47$ rank-one
reactions and the weakly-$T$-dependent discrete levels
are real structural properties of ENDF/B-VII.1 cross
sections, worth exploiting; the raw Ducru weights are
\emph{not} a partition of unity and must be normalized
before being applied to a $\log\sigma$ SVD. A fully
constrained kernel-Gram QP from~\cite{ducru2017}~§4 would
plausibly tighten the $213$~pcm off-library residual
further and is the single highest-value piece of future
work on this line. We report the positive, mixed, and
negative findings together so the trade-off is visible.
