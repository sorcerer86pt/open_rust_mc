\section{Conclusion}

Truncated SVD of the $\log_{10}$-transformed cross-section
matrix is a defensible drop-in representation for the
pointwise table. The rank-$5$ SVD provider reproduces the
ICSBEP HEU-MET-FAST-$001$ critical
$k_\text{eff} = 1.0000 \pm 100$~pcm
($1\sigma$)~\cite{icsbep_hmf001} to $+79 \pm 38$~pcm,
inside the experimental uncertainty band. OpenMC~$0.15.3$
on the same ENDF/B-VII.1 HDF5 files sits at $-99$~pcm,
also inside the band; the two codes straddle the
experimental value from opposite sides. The cross-code
spread to OpenMC ($+178$~pcm) is larger than either
code's offset from experiment and is reported as a
cross-code diagnostic, not as a deviation from the
physical benchmark.

On a nine-nuclide thermal PWR pin cell (no physical
benchmark exists; OpenMC~$0.15.3$ is the independent
code-to-code reference) the same engine running rank-$5$
SVD vs its own pointwise table agrees on $k_\infty$ within
$13$--$47$~pcm on two hardware tiers, and all four
in-engine providers (CPU table, CPU SVD, GPU pointwise,
GPU SVD) agree with OpenMC~$0.15.3$ to
$|\Delta| \le 51$~pcm at ten-seed statistics. A Humlicek-W4
Faddeeva evaluator, a pure-Rust WMP reader, and a SVD+WMP
hybrid provider all work end-to-end and agree with the
ACE+WMP industry baseline within $-26$~pcm at single-seed
statistics ($\ll 1\sigma_\text{seed}$). A CUDA port of the
WMP evaluator is bit-exact against the CPU path and
$7\times$ faster per lookup on a consumer GPU.

At a realistic \emph{off-library} operating temperature,
the story is more interesting. With a uniform $+150$~K
offset applied to every nuclide (fuel $900\!\to\!1050$~K,
moderator $600\!\to\!750$~K), OpenMC-style stochastic
$T$-picking enabled on the pointwise and hybrid paths, and
a three-point unity Ducru weight scheme on the SVD path
(Sec.~\ref{sec:ducru_puoi}), SVD reproduces $k_\infty$
within $213$~pcm of ACE+WMP at ten-seed $50\,000$-particle
$120$-batch statistics ($\sim\!3.6\sigma_\text{seed}$).
SVD is $22\%$ smaller than ACE+WMP in memory and matches
its speed within $0.07\%$. At a realistic off-library
operating temperature a compressed SVD representation of
ENDF/B-VII.1 cross sections is smaller than the industry
ACE+WMP baseline, ties it on speed, and trails it by a
characterisable small systematic on
$k_\infty$ (Sec.~\ref{sec:off_library_3t}).

A two-point unity Ducru ablation leaves a $2094$~pcm
kernel-approximation bias at the same operating conditions
(Sec.~\ref{sec:off_library_3t}); a simplex-projected QP
that clips the signed Ducru weights to non-negativity is
worse ($5\%$ L2 error on U-$238$ capture versus $0.84\%$
for the signed three-point scheme) because the simplex
projection is Euclidean-optimal in weight space whereas
the raw Ducru weights are Euclidean-optimal in the
Doppler-kernel norm (Sec.~\ref{sec:off_library_qp}).
Channel-consistent stochastic $T$-picking is required:
$\xi$ is drawn once per nuclide-collision and shared across
every partial channel, so the sum of partials stays at a
single sampled library temperature
(Sec.~\ref{sec:off_library_bug}); on-library measurements
do not exercise the pick.

The on-library performance picture is mixed.
CPU SVD rank-$5$ is $1.37\times$--$1.90\times$ faster than
the in-engine pointwise table on PWR pin cell
(hardware-dependent) and $1.43\times$ faster than the table
on Godiva on the large-L3 system; GPU SVD is $1.30\times$ \emph{slower}
than GPU pointwise on PWR because four clad nuclides require
summing $13$ discrete-level SVD kernels per lookup.
The hybrid is $2.06\times$ slower than CPU SVD because
WMP evaluation is $\sim\!15\times$ more expensive than the
SVD FMA per lookup.

The memory picture has two distinct components. The representation-byte ratio of
$132.9\times$ (WMP + smooth-SVD vs four-channel
six-temperature pointwise) is real; the live in-engine
reduction from the smooth-only SVD rebuild is
$1.06\times$ ($519.0 \to 487.6$~MB), and the hybrid engine
is absolutely $4.8$--$5.2\times$ larger than the
pointwise-table baseline. The discrete-level SVD kernels,
which WMP does not cover, dominate the engine's working
set. Reducing those kernels from rank $5$ to rank $1$ is a
zero-cost change because levels are weakly $T$-dependent
(Sec.~\ref{sec:discrete_rank}); it closes $\approx\!70\%$
of the engine's SVD working set on Godiva with no
measurable $k_\text{eff}$ shift. This is
what enables the off-library $22\%$ memory win against
ACE+WMP referenced above: with discrete-level rank $1$
applied, the SVD engine is absolutely smaller than the
two-endpoint stochastic-$T$ pointwise table even before
the WMP comparison.

Rank-$5$ SVD with three-point unity Ducru temperature
interpolation and discrete-level rank-$1$ reproduces the
ICSBEP Godiva experimental $k_\text{eff}$ within
$+79 \pm 38$~pcm (inside the $100$~pcm experimental
uncertainty), agrees with OpenMC and its own pointwise
baseline within $\le 3\,\mathrm{SEM}_{10}$ on PWR at
on-library $T$, and agrees with ACE+WMP within
$\sim\!3.6\,\mathrm{SEM}_{10}$ on PWR at off-library $T$.
On memory it is modestly smaller on-library and $22\%$
smaller off-library; on speed it is
$1.4\times$--$1.9\times$ faster than pointwise on CPU and
at parity with ACE+WMP off-library. The $43/47$ rank-one
reactions and the weakly-$T$-dependent discrete levels
are structural properties of ENDF/B-VII.1 cross sections;
the raw Ducru weights are not a partition of unity and
must be normalized before being applied to a $\log\sigma$
SVD.

The kernel-Gram QP from \cite{ducru2017}~§4 has a
structural subtlety relative to the signed unity scheme:
at low rank it wins (U-$238$ MT$=102$ $6.67$~eV peak ratio
$1.000$ at rank~$3$ vs $0.994$ for signed unity), but at
production rank~$5$ it is worse ($0.983$ vs $0.990$ on the
same peak). The QP's constrained optimum lies on the
boundary of the probability simplex --- one weight clips
to zero, collapsing the three-point problem to a two-point
one --- and at higher rank the signed unity scheme's
negative weight on the far-away library column acts as a
useful shape-cancellation term. Non-negativity is the
wrong constraint once basis-truncation error stops
dominating; signed three-point unity is the production
default at rank~$5$.
