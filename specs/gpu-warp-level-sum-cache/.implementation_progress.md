## Key result

The per-warp `(nuc, E_idx)` cache flagged in paper §gpu and §threats was
implemented end-to-end. Mechanism is correct (AC2: Δk_inf within SEM),
but the energy-bin sort (256 bins over 17 decades, 6% bin width)
provides insufficient warp coherence at the per-nuclide energy-grid
granularity, so the cache rarely activates and **does not close the
GPU SVD vs GPU pointwise gap on PWR pin cell**. The paper has been
updated to report this honestly.

Speedup: **1.003×** at 5-seed paper-scale (vs the predicted gap-closure
to ~1.30× of GPU pointwise). The hypothesis is empirically falsified
under the conditions tested; remediation directions (finer pre-sort,
per-thread cache, or a different discrete-level representation) are
identified but out of scope.
