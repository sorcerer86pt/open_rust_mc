=== Reference: textbook self-shielding magnitude ===
Bondarenko-like shielding factor for U-238 in standard PWR fuel
at URR (50 keV): in the 0.85-0.98 range (Sanchez 1981 Table II,
Stamm'ler 1983 §6.4, NJOY PURR module benchmarks).
Rust's effective factor: 0.3131
Rust's effective reduction: 68.7% (textbook: 2-15%)

=== Root cause hypothesis ===
The rational approximation sigma_eff = sigma_inf * sigma_0/(sigma_0+sigma_e)
is the Bondarenko shielding factor for the **resonance integral**, i.e.
the resonance-fluctuation part of the absorption XS above the smooth
baseline. In the URR, only the resonance ladder contributes to the
sample's deviation from smooth — potential elastic and smooth capture
should NOT be shielded.

Rust applies the factor to the FULL elastic, fission, and capture URR
samples — including the smooth baseline. Since the URR window of U-238
has elastic XS dominated by potential scattering (~9 b smooth) and
capture dominated by smooth s-wave (~0.3-0.5 b smooth), reducing them
by 69% over-shields by an order of magnitude.

Fix sketch: apply the factor only to (sigma_URR - sigma_smooth) with
sigma_smooth being the off-resonance interpolant. NJOY PURR uses the
Hwang superposition method; equivalent to keeping the dilute-limit
baseline and shielding only the resonance contribution.

Quick patch alternative: gate the correction on capture only (the
only channel where URR fluctuations dominate over smooth baseline)
— would cut the over-correction by ~10x and likely get within 50-200 pcm.
