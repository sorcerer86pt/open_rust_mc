# Reading:
# - At ~21 mfp analog gives ZERO transmitted in 500 k or 2 M histories
#   — the regime where naive WW saturates (max_split^N_voxels exponential
#   bound).
# - NEE last-only delivers FOM = 0.11 with σ_rel = 11.4% at 2 M — a
#   usable estimate where analog is unrecoverable. last-only is the
#   right estimator at thick slabs because the photon's actual final
#   forward-flight is the dominant escape channel.
# - first-only is a strict lower-bound estimator (≤ true T): it only
#   counts the contribution from the *first* Compton collision and
#   ignores everything that happens after, so deep paths that
#   contribute via late scatters are undercounted. Useful as a sanity
#   floor.
# - full per-coll sum is unbiased in expectation but suffers from
#   variance blowup at deep penetration: per-collision contributions
#   grow as (1/μ_z) · exp(-Σ_t · (T-z)/μ_z) — collisions near the
#   detector face contribute orders of magnitude more than collisions
#   near the source. Rare deep-penetration histories dominate the
#   sum and give σ_rel ~ 99% even at 2 M histories. Standard MCNP fix
#   is Russian roulette on small NEE contributions; future work.

# Conclusion:
# Task #5 ("DXTRAN-style point-detector estimator") is satisfied by the
# existing `--next-event` plumbing in `shield_slab` (Compton-channel
# next-event estimator with bound-corrected S(x,Z), 16-point GL on
# (μ_scat, ψ) integrand, MCNP exclusion-zone regularisation). At 300 cm
# water with 1 MeV photons, last-only NEE at 2 M histories gives FOM
# = 0.11 — analog gives FOM = 0. The infrastructure was previously
# documented as "research-tier work" in resume.md; it has been in tree

# kernel and shield_slab CLI.
